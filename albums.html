Born To Die

Lana Del Rey first major-label album was Born To Die, which came out in 2012. Although critics didn’t embrace the album, it reached No. 2 on the Billboard 200, has sold more than 7 million copies worldwide, and has been certified platinum five times. It remains her best-selling album to date and includes one of her most popular songs “Summertime Sadness.”

Lana Del Rey Announces New Album 'Lasso'

Lana Del Rey has announced her 10th studio album will be titled Lasso — fitting for the country sound she says it will embrace. She first teased new music early in January 2024, when she posted a snippet of an unreleased song titled “Henry, Come On” on Instagram — tagging producer Luke Laird, who has worked with country mainstays like Kacey Musgraves, Carrie Underwood and more.

Her move into the country genre follows her most recent release, a cover of John Denver’s 1971 hit “Take Me Home, Country Roads,” which she surprised fans with in December 2023.
